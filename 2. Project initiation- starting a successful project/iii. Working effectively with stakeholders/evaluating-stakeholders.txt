# Completing a stakeholder analysis
  - Primacy stakeholders are prople who will benefit directly from the project's success, while secondary stakeholders are indirectly impacted by the project's success.
  + 3 key steps for stakeholders analysis
    - Make a list of all the stakeholders the project impacts
    - Determine the level of interest and influence for each stakeholder
    - Asses stakeholders' ability to participate and then find ways to involve them
  + 4 methods for classification of stakeholders
    + Visualizing your analysis
      - meet their needs (top-left on chart) : high influence, low interest
      - Key player (top-right on chart) : high influence, high interest
      - Monitor (bottom-left on chart) : low influence, low interest
      - Show consideration (bottom-right) : low influence, high interest

# Prioritizing stakeholders and generating their buy-in
  + Key steps in stakeholder analysis:
    - Make a list and ask yourself: who is invested?, who is impacted?, and who contributes?
    - Determine the level of interest and influence for each stakeholder
    - Active stakeholder : ability to participate and find ways to involve them. Passive stakeholder : perfers high-level updates and not involves in day-to-day actions.
  + Steering committee : collection of key stakeholders who have a high level of power and interest in a project
