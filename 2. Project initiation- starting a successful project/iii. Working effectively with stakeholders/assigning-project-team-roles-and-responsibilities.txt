# Elements of a RACI chart
  + RACI chart
    - A RACI chart helps to define roles and responsibilities for individuals or teams to ensure work gets done efficiently
  + RACI = Responsible, Accountable, Consulted, Informed

# Building out a RACI chart
  + Elements on RACI chart:
    - R: Responsible: who gets the work done
    - A: Accountable: who makes sure the work is done
    - C: Consulted: who gives input or feedback on work
    - I: Informed: who needs to know the outcome

# Certificate completers: Staying motivated in the program

# Getting the most out of a RACI chart
  + Workload balance 
    - Review R assignments to identify overloaded stakeholders and balance responsibilities.
    - Prevent knowledge silos by distributing responsibilities across multiple team members.
    - Delegate tasks effectively to reduce burnout and maintain a balanced workload.
  + Put your RACI into practice
    - Share the RACI chart to gain stakeholder buy-in and clear responsibility alignment.
    - Document stakeholder acknowledgment through the project charter, meeting notes, or RACI chart.
    - Assign clear task ownership to improve communication and decision-making throughout the project.  
  + When should you use a RACI chart?
    - Use a RACI chart for large, complex projects with many stakeholders.
    - Small projects with clear roles may not benefit from a RACI chart.
    - Creating a RACI chart improves project understanding and management experience
